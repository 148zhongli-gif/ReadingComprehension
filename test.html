“The Lost Puppy”

Mira was walking home from school when she heard a soft whimper behind a tree. Curious, she looked closer and saw a tiny brown puppy shaking in the cold. The puppy had no collar and looked scared, but it wagged its tail when it saw her.

Mira picked it up gently. The puppy felt light and warm in her arms. She wondered who it belonged to, and she decided to check the neighborhood. She knocked on several doors, but no one said they were missing a dog.

Not wanting to leave the puppy alone, Mira took it home. Her mother agreed they could care for it until the owner was found. Mira smiled as the puppy curled up on her lap. For the first time that day, she felt warmth too.

“The Last Train North”

Darian boarded the last train heading north, a route known for crossing empty landscapes where towns had long been abandoned. He carried nothing but a backpack and a journal filled with unfinished stories. He wasn’t running from anything—just searching for a place quiet enough to hear his own thoughts.

As the train moved deeper into the wilderness, Darian noticed another passenger: an elderly woman knitting calmly. She occasionally glanced at him with knowing eyes. When the train stopped at a deserted station, she finally spoke, telling him that everyone who rides this route is looking for something—answers, peace, or a new beginning.

Darian realized that the journey itself was giving him the clarity he needed. He opened his journal and began writing again, the words flowing as the train rolled through the silent mountains. Some journeys, he understood, are meant to be taken not to reach a destination but to rediscover oneself.
